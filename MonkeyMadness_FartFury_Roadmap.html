💨 Gameplay Roadmap · Mateo's Arcade · v2.0

Monkey Madness:
Fart Fury

The original was a tidy little dodge game. This is the sequel that weaponises the joke: flatulent monkeys rocket bananas at you out of their butts, you fart to jump, dodge, and blast them back. Every action makes a noise, pulls a face, and leaves a mess. Built belly-laughs first, skill second.

🎯 Audience kids 4–8 + parents 📱 iOS SpriteKit · iPhone/iPad ⏱ Runs 60–120s 🏆 Keeps the "$10 in your bank!" win
01 The North Star

Three pillars everything hangs off

If a feature doesn't serve one of these, it doesn't ship. This is the filter for every "should we add…?" question later.

🚀

The fart is the engine

Propulsion, weapon, comedy, and feedback all come from gas. If it moves, it farted to get there — monkeys, bananas, the player, the boss.

🧃

Maximum juice

Squash & stretch, screen shake, googly-eye wobble, stink clouds. Small toot → small pop; mega-fart → screen-wide chaos. Feedback always matches the weight of the action.

🔁

One-more-fart loop

Short runs, instant restart, escalating absurdity, and light collectibles so a 6-year-old and a parent keep tapping "again".

02 The Core Reframe

From "dodge bananas" to "fart combat"

Keep the proven spine — monkeys overhead, player on the ground, parabolic bananas, half-screen touch zones. Then turn every beat into a gag and give the player their own gas.

🐒💨Monkeys fart bananasEach throw is a butt-rocket: "PBBBT", a trail, and recoil that shoves the monkey back.
🧒💨Player fart-jumpsJump = a parp on takeoff + poof cloud on landing. Double-tap = double-toot.
⬇️💥Fart-slam to deflectFart downward to bounce bananas back and stun a monkey — the skill layer.
🌫️😷The mess mattersLingering stink clouds slow you if you run through them. Risk vs. reward, comedy either way.
Why it works: the original mechanic is intact, so it's still simple enough for a 4-year-old — but now there's a two-way verb (dodge and counter), which is what turns a one-note toy into something with a skill ceiling parents will fight over.
03 Gameplay Directions

Four ways this game could go

These are distinct visions, not features to stack. Pick the spine first — you can graft the others on later as unlockable modes.

DIRECTION A

The Great Dodge

Verb: survive

Evolve the original, straight. Pure dodging across 10 juiced levels + boss, plus an endless mode. Lowest risk, closest to the brief, ships fastest.

Pros
  • Fastest to build
  • Matches existing brief 1:1
Cons
  • Least novel
  • Thin replay on its own
◆ Recommended core DIRECTION B

Fart Back!

Verb: dodge + counter

The player farts to deflect bananas and stun monkeys. Dodge to survive, counter to win faster. Fart duels with the boss. Same simplicity, far funnier, real skill ceiling.

Pros
  • Huge comedy + replay
  • Still one-thumb simple
Cons
  • More tuning of deflect timing
DIRECTION C

Fart Jetpack Climber

Verb: ascend

Hold to fart and fly up through the jungle canopy, dodging monkey snipers on the branches. Doodle-Jump energy with a gas tank. Great as a second mode.

Pros
  • Novel, addictive chase
  • Natural score-chasing
Cons
  • New art + vertical camera
DIRECTION D

Monkey Mayhem Arena

Verb: knock about

Ragdoll monkeys, fart-powered knockback, chaos sandbox with optional 2-player-on-one-iPad. "Gang Beasts for kids." The most shareable, the hardest to nail.

Pros
  • Hilarious, viral clips
  • Couch co-op fun
Cons
  • Physics tuning is deep
  • Biggest build
My recommendation: Build A's scope as the MVP but design it as B (Fart Back) from day one — ship the dodge core, then layer the player-fart counter. Bank C and D as unlockable modes for later updates so you always have "new stuff" to bring kids back.

05 The Comedy Toolkit

How you actually make it laughable

"Funny" isn't a feature you tick — it's the sum of dozens of tiny reactions. Sound does most of the heavy lifting; faces and mess do the rest.

🔊
Sound is 70% of the joke

A whole library of farts — squeaky, tuba, trumpet, machine-gun, the sad deflating-balloon on death. Randomise so it never repeats twice; pitch rises with banana speed. This single system carries the humour.

😣
Faces that telegraph and react

Monkeys strain (😣) before a fart, relief (😌) after. Player pulls a shocked 😮 on a near-miss. Googly eyes wobble on every impact — free personality on top of the emoji base.

🌫️
Stink clouds you can trip over

Green puff particles linger with wavy stink-lines and the odd fly 🪰. Run through one and you cough and slow — a funny risk baked into the level.

🌀
Ragdoll recoil

Monkeys spin and tumble from their own blasts; the boss gets rocketed backward. Newton's third law, but make it gross.

🌪️
"Fart Storm" random modifiers

Every ~20s a silly rule flips on: Bean Burrito (everyone double-farts), Silent-But-Deadly (invisible bananas, only a stink cloud warns you), Baby Monkey (tiny squeaks), Slow-Mo Toot. Variety = the reason to keep playing.

🚀
Even dying is funny

Lose your last life and the kid farts himself into orbit with a Wilhelm-scream parp. "Game Over" should get a laugh, not a frown.

06 Rich Graphics

From flat emoji to juicy cartoon

The brief keeps emoji as the readable base — smart, keep it. Then layer richness on top so it reads as a real cartoon, not a text string.

🎨

Emoji base + vector overlays

Keep the instantly-readable emoji, but add hand-drawn eyes, puffing cheeks, butt-jet flames and motion trails as sprite overlays.

🏞️

Parallax jungle

3–4 depth layers (far mountains, mid canopy, near leaves) drifting at different speeds for real depth and life behind the action.

💡

Dynamic lighting & glow

Fart clouds glow toxic-green, bananas carry a warm rim light, the boss casts a shadow. Cheap tricks, big richness.

✨

A real particle system

Puffs, peels, sweat drops, dust-on-landing, confetti, stink swirls — SKEmitterNode makes this near-free on iPhone.

🫨

Squash, stretch & wobble

Every character and every UI button squishes and springs. Hearts throb, the combo counter pops and scales. Nothing sits still.

📳

Haptics = feel

A little buzz on every fart and hit. On iPhone this is the difference between "cute" and "satisfying" — and it's a couple of lines of code.

Art direction stays on-brief: "Saturday-morning cartoon meets emoji" — thick outlines, high saturation, the canonical night-jungle palette pushed a little brighter and gassier.

07 The Addiction Loop

Why they tap "again" (and again)

Hyper-casual games hook through a tight "tap → reward → repeat" loop with a light layer of progression on top. Here's that loop, monkey-flavoured.

⚡
Instant restart

No menus between runs — death screen reads "TAP TO FART AGAIN" and drops you straight back in. Friction kills the loop.

🔥
Combos & near-misses score big

Dodge-streak multipliers, deflect bonuses, and a "Butt Cheek" bonus for surviving a razor-thin near-miss. Big juicy numbers pop on every one.

🪙
Banana Coins → cosmetic unlocks

Earn coins by playing; spend on fart skins (rainbow, glitter, fire), monkey hats, and new arenas. Light meta-progression is exactly what 2025 hyper-casual games add to lift retention — with zero pay-to-win.

🧑‍🚀
Unlockable characters

Different heroes — a girl, a dog, grandma — each with a signature fart and stat quirk. "Just one more to unlock the dog."

📅
Daily "Fart of the Day"

A rotating daily challenge modifier + a small reward. A reason to open the app tomorrow, not just today.

🏆
Keep the big win moment

The "$10 in your bank account!" celebration is pure gold for Mateo — keep it. Add ranks around it: Fart Cadet → Toot Master → Gas Guardian.

09 The Build Roadmap

Ship in phases, prove the fun early

Estimates assume one developer. The whole point of Phase 0 is to answer one question cheaply: is the fart actually funny? If yes, everything else is worth building.

Phase 0 · Prototype~1–2 weeks
Goal: prove the core gag is funny
  • SpriteKit skeleton: juiced dodge core + player fart-jump
  • One monkey, one banana, the first 5 fart sounds + haptics
  • Squash/stretch + screen shake on every hit
  • Playtest with Mateo. Laughs = green light.
Phase 1 · MVP "Fart Back" slice~3–4 weeks
Goal: a complete, funny, shippable loop
  • Full juice pass; 3 monkey types; the deflect/Butt-Slam mechanic
  • 10 levels + King Kong-a-Toot boss; win/lose/level-complete screens
  • Core fart-SFX library; combo scoring; the "$10" win moment
Phase 2 · Rich graphics + retention~3–4 weeks
Goal: make it beautiful and sticky
  • Parallax jungle, particle system, dynamic lighting/glow
  • Banana Coins, cosmetic fart-skins, unlockable characters
  • Daily "Fart of the Day" + Fart Storm random events
Phase 3 · Modes + App Store polishongoing
Goal: give kids a reason to keep coming back
  • Jetpack Climber mode (Direction C); Arena/co-op experiments (D)
  • More arenas & characters as free updates
  • App icon, splash, and a laugh-out-loud App Preview video
10 Foundations

Tech stack & keeping it kid-safe

🛠️ Recommended stack

  • SpriteKit — as the brief suggests. Built-in particles (SKEmitterNode), SKAction for squash/stretch, physics for ragdoll & deflect.
  • AVAudioEngine — layered, pitch-shifted fart playback so sounds never feel canned.
  • Core Haptics — a buzz per fart/hit; the cheapest "feel" win on iPhone.
  • SwiftUI — menus, shop, unlock screens around the SpriteKit scene.
  • Keep the proven half-screen touch zones; add hold-to-charge for the mega-fart.

🛡️ Tone & safety guardrails

  • Gross-out stays gentle: cartoon gas + bananas only. No toilets, no real poop, no mean-spirited humour.
  • Monkeys are silly, never scary — googly eyes, not menace.
  • No ads, no IAP for a 6-year-old's game. Every unlock is earned by playing. COPPA-clean, parent-friendly.
  • Always-positive win language; death is a joke, not a punishment.
