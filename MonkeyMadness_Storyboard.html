The Win

"$10 in your bank!" — keep this hero beat.

Tension & release, not a straight ramp. Bursts of intensity (peaks) with brief breather waves (dips) so players can catch a breath — then the boss spike at the end. Research shows a mix of fast and slow beats keeps players in flow far better than a monotonic climb.

03 Design specs — what's coming, and how

The next builds, research-backed

🎁

Power-ups

✓ Shipped

Temporary abilities that drop from stunned monkeys — so offense (farting back) feeds your defense. Each one answers a specific threat, and grabbing it is a risk (you leave your safe spot).

😷 Gas Mask

~6s shield — walk through one hit. Counters black/boomer blasts.

🥇 Golden Banana

Score ×2 for ~8s. Pure reward — bait to chase into danger.

👃 Nose Plug

Slow-mo ~4s — read a chaotic screen. Counters Marmoset spam.

🫧 Beano

Clears all stink clouds + peels on screen. Panic button.

  • Risk/reward: drops fall to the ground and expire — grabbing one means leaving cover, so it's a real choice, not a freebie.
  • One at a time: no stacking buffs; keeps it readable for a 6-year-old.
  • Loud feedback: a sticker pop + jingle on pickup; a clear on-body aura while active; a blink before it runs out.

Research: rewards need a risk-reward trade-off, and each power-up should exist to help overcome a specific obstacle — not be handed out freely.

👑

Boss: King Kong-a-Toot

✓ Shipped

A giant gorilla in a crown, hanging from the whole branch. Three distinct phases — each a different puzzle, not just "more bananas". You damage it by farting its own bananas back.

Phase 1 — Aimed

Slow, single well-telegraphed shots. Teaches the fart-back rhythm.

Phase 2 — Spread

Wide fart-fans; use the barrier + gaps. Movement puzzle.

Phase 3 — Panic

Faster, mixes peels + minions. All your tools at once.

  • Telegraph everything: a big wind-up (like the Boomer's !!), colour + sound cue before each attack. Punish bad timing, never bad eyesight.
  • Phases feel different, not just harder — new attack, new counter each time.
  • Readable HP bar with juicy chunk-off on each hit; screen-flash + hitstop on phase change.

Research: telegraphs are essential; each attack should look/feel distinct; phase changes should be different, not merely harder.

📈

Progression & retention

Queued

Turn the endless loop into a game with shape and reasons to return — without adding cognitive load for a young player.

10 levels → boss

Structured arc (from the brief). Level-complete interstitials.

🪙 Banana Coins

Earn by playing → spend on cosmetics. No pay-to-win.

Cosmetic unlocks

Fart-skins (rainbow/fire/glitter), hats, characters, arenas.

📅 Fart of the Day

A daily challenge modifier + small reward. A reason to return tomorrow.

  • Short + long goals: per-run objectives and slow unlocks — games with only one underperform on both day-1 and day-30 return.
  • Gradual unlocks: reveal features over time for discovery and less overwhelm.
  • Show, don't nag: progress bars + reward previews, never intrusive pop-ups. Keep the "$10 in your bank!" as the emotional peak.

Research: unpredictable rewards lift return rate ~70%; balancing short-term & long-term goals drives both early and late retention.

🎚️

Difficulty & pacing

Tuning pass

Make the climb feel fair and thrilling, with breathers built in.

  • Breather waves: after an intense burst, a calmer wave (fewer/slower monkeys) so the player exhales — then ramp again.
  • Rebalance the Marmoset: its rapid small bananas currently cost a full life each — likely too punishing. Move to chip-damage or pure dodge-pressure.
  • Gentle catch-up (optional DDA): if a young player keeps dying early, quietly ease the next run a touch. Keep it invisible.
  • Lock the win state: revisit whether slipping on a peel should cost a life (currently just a stumble).

Research: pace with tension and release — challenging stretches followed by easier ones — to hold players in flow.

05 The Swift gate

When & how we port

Port when fun-complete

  • Trigger: gates 4–6 done (power-ups, levels+boss, tuned/theme locked).
  • Target: SpriteKit (per the design brief) + SwiftUI menus.
  • The HTML uses the same physics constants + half-screen touch model, so it's a faithful translation, not a redesign.

One hedge: a Swift "feel spike"

  • A ~half-day spike: one screen, player + one monkey, touch + haptics on a real iPhone.
  • De-risks the only things HTML can't validate: true device touch feel, haptics, audio latency, particle perf.
  • Do it in parallel before the full port — not now.

Step off the line and you're safe; stand on it and you're not; bonking it mid-aim cancels the shot. From level 6 — the first enemy that punishes standing still rather than being in the wrong place. Plus iOS haptics (bonk / hurt / pickup / lock-warning / boss), generators pre-warmed so the buzz lands on the beat.

Every archive would have shipped as build 1 and been rejected as a duplicate. Now wired through — TestFlight builds are 1.1 (2) onward.

Difficulty is unaffected — monkey throws are timed to a flight duration, not a speed — and the fart cloud's rise is now derived from the field height so it still reaches the branch in ~1.5s on any screen.
