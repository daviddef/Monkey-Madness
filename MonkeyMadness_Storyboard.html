The Win

"$10 in your bank!" — the hero beat, kept.

Tension & release, not a straight ramp. Bursts of intensity (peaks) with brief breather waves (dips) so players can catch a breath — then the boss spike at the end. Research shows a mix of fast and slow beats keeps players in flow far better than a monotonic climb.

03 Design specs — how the shipped systems work

The research behind what's built

🎁

Power-ups

✓ Shipped

Temporary abilities that drop from stunned monkeys — so offense (farting back) feeds your defense. Each one answers a specific threat, and grabbing it is a risk (you leave your safe spot).

😷 Gas Mask

~6s shield — walk through one hit. Counters black/boomer blasts.

🥇 Golden Banana

Score ×2 for ~8s. Pure reward — bait to chase into danger.

👃 Nose Plug

Slow-mo ~4s — read a chaotic screen. Counters Marmoset spam.

🫧 Beano

Clears all stink clouds + peels on screen. Panic button.

  • Risk/reward: drops fall to the ground and expire — grabbing one means leaving cover, so it's a real choice, not a freebie.
  • One at a time: no stacking buffs; keeps it readable for a 6-year-old.
  • Loud feedback: a sticker pop + jingle on pickup; a clear on-body aura while active; a blink before it runs out.

Research: rewards need a risk-reward trade-off, and each power-up should exist to help overcome a specific obstacle — not be handed out freely.

👑

Boss: King Kong-a-Toot

✓ Shipped

A giant gorilla in a crown, hanging from the whole branch. Three distinct phases — each a different puzzle, not just "more bananas". You damage it by farting its own bananas back.

Phase 1 — Aimed

Slow, single well-telegraphed shots. Teaches the fart-back rhythm.

Phase 2 — Spread

Wide fart-fans; use the barrier + gaps. Movement puzzle.

Phase 3 — Panic

Faster, mixes peels + minions. All your tools at once.

  • Telegraph everything: a big wind-up (like the Boomer's !!), colour + sound cue before each attack. Punish bad timing, never bad eyesight.
  • Phases feel different, not just harder — new attack, new counter each time.
  • Readable HP bar with juicy chunk-off on each hit; screen-flash + hitstop on phase change.

Research: telegraphs are essential; each attack should look/feel distinct; phase changes should be different, not merely harder.

📈

Progression & retention

✓ Shipped

Turn the endless loop into a game with shape and reasons to return — without adding cognitive load for a young player.

10 levels → boss

Structured arc (from the brief). Level-complete interstitials.

🪙 Banana Coins

Earn by playing → spend on cosmetics. No pay-to-win.

Cosmetic unlocks

Fart-skins (rainbow/fire/glitter), hats, characters, arenas.

📅 Fart of the Day

A daily challenge modifier + small reward. A reason to return tomorrow.

  • Short + long goals: per-run objectives and slow unlocks — games with only one underperform on both day-1 and day-30 return.
  • Gradual unlocks: reveal features over time for discovery and less overwhelm.
  • Show, don't nag: progress bars + reward previews, never intrusive pop-ups. Keep the "$10 in your bank!" as the emotional peak.

Research: unpredictable rewards lift return rate ~70%; balancing short-term & long-term goals drives both early and late retention.

🎚️

Difficulty & pacing

Tuning pass

Make the climb feel fair and thrilling, with breathers built in.

  • Breather waves: after an intense burst, a calmer wave (fewer/slower monkeys) so the player exhales — then ramp again.
  • Rebalance the Marmoset: its rapid small bananas currently cost a full life each — likely too punishing. Move to chip-damage or pure dodge-pressure.
  • Gentle catch-up (optional DDA): if a young player keeps dying early, quietly ease the next run a touch. Keep it invisible.
  • Lock the win state: revisit whether slipping on a peel should cost a life (currently just a stumble).

Research: pace with tension and release — challenging stretches followed by easier ones — to hold players in flow.

Free Play — a no-fail mode

✓ Built · lab + iOS

A calm entry where nothing can go wrong: no lives, no game-over, just fart around with the monkeys. Young kids often bounce off a fail state — this lets him mess about, learn the moves, and still earn coins for the shop. Cheap: it reuses every spawn and system, just switches off death and the OVER screen.

No lives

Still earns coins

So it feeds the same shop — messing about isn't a dead end.

Gentle mix

Leans on the Easy barrage; no boss pressure, no clock.

One tap from the menu

A quiet toggle by PLAY, not a mode-select wall.

Research: for the youngest players, a no-lose sandbox raises session count and lowers rage-quits — the fun is the toy, not the win state.

Controls that fit a small thumb

✓ Built · lab + iOS

Straight from watching him play. Two changes, both aimed at a six-year-old's hands.

Zones is the default now

Touch a side to steer, tap to jump — no more five little ◀▶ targets. A saved choice still wins, so Settings can pick Buttons back.

The steering zone is drawn

Two faint dotted boxes over the lower third show exactly where left/right responds — and each lights up while that side is held. No more invisible touch area.

David's note: "the default not to be arrows" and "draw faint dotted lines around the area that's sensitive to my left/right." Both done.

🛡️

Carried power-ups — buy, load, fire

✓ Built · lab + iOS

A new POWER-UPS row in the Banana Shop, opening with the Shield (150 🍌). Different from the drops that fall off stunned monkeys: these are yours, on demand — a clutch save a kid can reach for. Built as a list, so adding the next one is a single line; the shop row, the button and the charges all read off it.

One button, two jobs

A single tap rotates which power-up is loaded; a double tap fires it. No sixth control on an already-tight strip.

The ordering trap, avoided

The rotate is deferred 300ms, so a double tap can't rotate first and then fire the wrong thing. Firing is instant on the second tap.

One charge per level

Owned forever, re-armed at every level and the boss. Generous enough to actually use; it still only blocks one hit.

Appears only when owned

A new player gets no dead control, and the Buttons strip stays clear until there's a reason for it.

Verified: double-tap fires and spends the charge with the pending rotate cancelled; a single tap fires nothing and leaves the charge alone; an empty charge is refused.

06 How it's built

The lab → Swift pipeline (settled)

✅ Ported — and the port is done

  • Went with UIKit + CADisplayLink + Core Graphics, not SpriteKit — a near 1:1 translation of the canvas renderer, so the HTML stays a faithful lab rather than a mock-up.
  • Same constants, same physics, same touch model. The logical canvas now takes the device's real aspect ratio (inset for the Dynamic Island) instead of letterboxing.
  • Audio: AVAudioEngine + a varispeed player pool over 8 real fart samples, pitched per theme.

The workflow that actually worked

  • Iterate in HTML, verify in the browser harness, then port. Logic bugs are far cheaper to catch there than in Xcode.
  • Screenshot every theme. A compile proves nothing about art — the black letterbox bands, the emoji-arrow fallback and the invisible "GAS" label all passed BUILD SUCCEEDED.
  • Don't trust config. project.yml's version was silently ignored for 3 builds; only reading the archive's own Info.plist caught it.

v22Why ◀▶ was unreliable — found it (David: "sometimes works other times not"). Not the game's code: iOS was eating the touches. The steering zone runs to the bottom of the canvas, which sits ~34pt from the screen edge — inside the home-indicator gesture region, where the system holds the first touch to check whether it's a home swipe and only passes it on if it isn't. Held presses get delayed or cancelled outright, which is exactly "sometimes". It hit ◀▶ hardest because they're held, low, right where a thumb rests — and my earlier fix widening the pad to the bottom edge made it worse. Fixed with preferredScreenEdgesDeferringSystemGestures = .bottom, so the game gets the first touch. Plus DOUBLE JUMP: a second mid-air parp, free on purpose (hover is the thing that costs gas). Measured: 140px → 242px at the apex, capped at 2, and it still can't reach the branch.
